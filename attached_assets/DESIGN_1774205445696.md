# Design System Specification: High-Performance Growth OS

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
### The Creative North Star: "The Digital Sanctuary"
This design system rejects the cluttered, "noisy" aesthetic of traditional productivity tools. Instead, it embraces **The Digital Sanctuary**â€”a space where focus is protected and progress is celebrated. We move beyond generic dark modes by utilizing a "Deep Space" depth model.

**Editorial Direction:**
To break the "template" look, we employ **Intentional Asymmetry**. Important metrics or "North Star" goals should occupy oversized, off-center display areas, while utility tasks are tucked into sophisticated, nested containers. We utilize high-contrast typography scalesâ€”pairing massive, thin display type with tiny, high-contrast labelsâ€”to create a sense of premium editorial authority.

## 2. Colors & Surface Architecture
The palette is rooted in `#0e0e0e` (Surface/Background) to provide a true-black inkiness that makes vibrant status accents pop with neon-like precision.

### The "No-Line" Rule
**Borders are forbidden for layout sectioning.** To separate a sidebar from a main feed, or a header from a content area, use a background shift from `surface` to `surface_container_low`. Boundaries must be felt, not seen.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Depth is achieved through a "Material Stack." Imagine sheets of obsidian and frosted glass:
1.  **Base Layer:** `surface` (#0e0e0e) - The infinite canvas.
2.  **Sectional Layer:** `surface_container_low` (#131313) - Large layout blocks.
3.  **Component Layer:** `surface_container` (#1a1a1a) or `surface_container_high` (#20201f) - Individual cards or modules.
4.  **Active/Floating Layer:** `surface_bright` (#2c2c2c) - Items requiring immediate focus.

### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
Floating overlays (modals, dropdowns) must use **Glassmorphism**. 
*   **Fill:** `surface_variant` (#262626) at 60% opacity.
*   **Effect:** `backdrop-blur: 20px`.
*   **Signature Texture:** Main Action CTAs should use a linear gradient from `primary` (#94aaff) to `primary_container` (#809bff) at a 135-degree angle to create a "liquid light" effect.

---

## 3. Typography
We pair **Manrope** (Display/Headlines) for its modern, geometric soul with **Inter** (Body/UI) for its world-class legibility.

*   **Display (Manrope):** Used for "Motivation Moments"â€”daily streaks or high-level goals. The scale is aggressive (up to 3.5rem) to create a sense of importance.
*   **Headlines (Manrope):** Bold and authoritative. Always use `on_surface` (#ffffff) to ensure they anchor the page.
*   **Body (Inter):** Set in `body-md` for standard reading. Use `on_surface_variant` (#adaaaa) for secondary descriptions to reduce visual fatigue.
*   **Labels (Inter):** Small, uppercase, and tracked out (+5% to +10%) for a technical, high-end "instrument panel" feel.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth
In this system, light is the primary architect.

### The Layering Principle
Avoid "Drop Shadows" in the traditional sense. Use **Tonal Layering**:
*   To make a card "pop," place a `surface_container_highest` card on a `surface_container_low` background. The subtle shift in gray creates a cleaner "lift" than a black shadow.

### Ambient Shadows & Ghost Borders
*   **Ambient Shadows:** For floating elements only. Use `shadow-xl` logic: Blur 40px, Spread -10px, Color: `rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.5)`.
*   **The Ghost Border:** For accessibility on interactive elements, use a 1px border of `outline_variant` (#484847) at **15% opacity**. It should be almost invisible until the user focuses on the element.
*   **Inner Glow:** Components like Primary Buttons should have a 1px "Inner Glow" on the top edge using a lighter tint of the accent color to simulate a physical edge catching light.

---

## 5. Components

### Buttons & Inputs
*   **Primary Action:** Gradient fill (`primary` to `primary_container`). `xl` roundedness (1.5rem). No border.
*   **Secondary/Ghost:** No fill. `Ghost Border` (15% opacity `outline_variant`).
*   **Input Fields:** `surface_container_highest` fill. No border. On focus, a subtle `primary` inner glow.

### Cards & Lists
*   **The Divider Ban:** Never use a line to separate list items. Use **Vertical Space** (Spacing `4` or `1rem`) or alternating tonal backgrounds (`surface_container_low` vs `surface_container`).
*   **Card Radius:** Always use `lg` (1rem) or `xl` (1.5rem) to maintain the "Modern & Minimal" friendliness.

### Status Indicators (High-Performance Accents)
*   **Tasks (Electric Blue):** Use `primary` (#94aaff).
*   **Completion (Emerald):** Use `secondary` (#5cfd80).
*   **Follow-ups (Amber):** Use `tertiary` (#ffbd5c).
*   **Missed/Error (Crimson):** Use `error` (#ff6e84).
*   *Implementation Note:* These colors should be used as "Glow" accents (small pips or subtle 2px left-border accents) rather than large blocks of color.

## 6. Doâ€™s and Donâ€™ts

### Do:
*   **DO** use whitespace as a functional tool. If a screen feels "busy," increase spacing rather than adding lines.
*   **DO** use `surface_container_lowest` (#000000) for deep-set areas like search bars or code blocks to create a "void" effect.
*   **DO** apply `9999px` (Full) rounding to status chips, but keep cards at `12px-24px`.

### Donâ€™t:
*   **DON'T** use pure white text (#ffffff) for long-form body copy; use `on_surface_variant` (#adaaaa) to prevent "halo" eye strain on dark backgrounds.
*   **DON'T** use 100% opaque borders. They break the "Sanctuary" feel and make the UI look like a legacy spreadsheet.
*   **DON'T** use standard easing. All transitions should use `cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0, 1)`â€”a "snappy yet smooth" motion that feels high-performance.
